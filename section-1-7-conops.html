1.7

Concept of Operations

1.7.1   Mission Overview

The mission deploys a probe-class lander and integrated compact rover to the lunar south pole to characterize the in-situ environmental effects of exploration activities within permanently shadowed regions (PSRs). The system operates on the lunar surface for one full lunar cycle (approximately 29.5 days), directly measuring dust accumulation, cryogenic thermal variation, and ionizing radiation flux in support of the STM science objectives under Sci-A-1 and HS-01. All acquired science data are returned to Earth to establish baseline environmental characterization for future human and robotic exploration of the PSR.

Constraint check: 3 sentences. No personal pronouns. Scope and location explicit.

1.7.2   System Architecture

The spacecraft consists of a single-element probe-class lander with an integrated compact rover, selected to minimize total wet mass while accommodating the primary science instrument suite. The lander provides the descent propulsion and landing structure, power generation and storage, and the primary communications link to Earth, while the rover delivers surface mobility and in-situ science data collection across the PSR transect. Key high-level system elements include the descent stage, rover mobility platform, science payload suite (dust sensor, thermal sensor array, and radiation dosimeter package), solar power subsystem with battery backup, and a command and data handling (CDH) and communications subsystem.

Constraint check: 3 sentences. High-level elements named, no granular design detail.

1.7.3   Operational Sequence

The mission launches as a secondary payload on a commercial rideshare vehicle, which delivers the spacecraft on a trans-lunar injection (TLI) trajectory toward the Moon. During the cruise phase, the spacecraft operates in a low-power safe mode with periodic health-check downlinks to the ground team. Upon arrival, the spacecraft performs lunar orbit insertion and a series of orbit reduction maneuvers to achieve the south pole approach corridor, followed by powered descent and soft landing at the target PSR boundary site. Following landing confirmation, the rover deploys from the lander deck, completes a surface checkout sequence, and initiates primary science operations. The rover then traverses prescribed transects over the 29.5-day operational period, collecting synchronized dust, thermal, and radiation measurements across the PSR illumination gradient, with science data downlinked to Earth via direct-to-Earth or relay-assisted link within 72 hours of acquisition. The mission concludes at end of the operational lifespan with final data transmission and subsystem safe power-down.

Operational Sequence — Step by Step

1
Launch & TLI
Spacecraft launches as secondary rideshare payload and is delivered on a trans-lunar injection trajectory. System enters low-power safe mode with periodic health-check downlinks.
2
Cruise
Trajectory correction maneuvers (TCMs) executed as required. Science instruments remain stowed. Ground team monitors telemetry via direct-to-Earth link.
3
Lunar Orbit Insertion + Powered Descent
Spacecraft performs LOI burn and orbit reduction maneuvers to achieve south pole approach corridor. Powered descent initiated; soft landing executed at target PSR boundary site.
4
Surface Checkout
Rover deploys from lander deck. Mobility, instrument power-on, and communications link verified. Initial housekeeping telemetry downlinked to ground.
5
Primary Science Operations — 29.5 Days
Rover traverses PSR transects collecting synchronized dust, thermal, and radiation measurements across the illumination gradient. Science data downlinked within 72 hours of acquisition via DTE or relay-assisted link. Mission concludes with final data return and safe power-down.

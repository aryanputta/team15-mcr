1.6   CDH Subsystem Selection Criteria

Command and Data Handling. The CDH subsystem provides the command authority, onboard autonomy, telemetry management, science-data storage, and communications interface for the Team 15 lunar south pole mission. It must preserve command execution and acknowledgment through cruise, descent, surface checkout, and PSR operations, while supporting low-power safe mode and autonomous fault response when real-time command is unavailable. The Brain notes already identify the governing drivers for this role: radiation-driven upset risk in avionics, intermittent or delayed communications, and the requirement to return primary science data within 72 hours of acquisition when a link is available. In this environment, the CDH selection must favor reliable packet handling, memory retention, and fault containment over feature count or software complexity.

Selection Criteria. The preferred CDH architecture shall fit within the verified rover and lander interfaces, including power, mass, volume, thermal, and wiring constraints, without introducing unnecessary integration risk. It shall remain functional through PSR thermal extremes, radiation exposure, dust contamination, and launch or landing shock, using radiation-tolerant components, watchdog-driven recovery, and error-correcting memory where required by the mission baseline. The design should support intermittent-contact operations through direct-to-Earth or relay-assisted downlink and protect buffered science data until a valid contact window opens. Where a threshold is not fixed in the Brain-derived mission baseline, it shall remain `[TBR]` rather than being assumed.

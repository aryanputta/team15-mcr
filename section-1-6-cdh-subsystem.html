1.6   CDH Subsystem Selection Criteria

The CDH subsystem shall be selected on the basis of whether it can preserve command authority, safeguard science data, and recover autonomously through the lunar south pole PSR environment using only the Brain-derived evidence already collected for Team 15. The working evidence set is narrow and specific: radiation-driven upset risk in avionics, intermittent or delayed communications, low-power survival and operations across the 29.5-day surface campaign, and the need to return primary science data within 72 hours of acquisition when a link is available. Because the mission depends on reliable packet handling, onboard storage, fault management, and downlink closure, the evaluation must emphasize command integrity and data continuity over raw subsystem feature count. Any unverified numerical threshold shall remain `[TBR]` until it is backed by the mission baseline or a formal analysis.

Primary Evaluation Criteria
Criterion Decision Focus Evidence Already Collected
Command integrity Preserve command authorization, execution, and acknowledgment through cruise, descent, checkout, and PSR operations. Brain evidence: cruise operations require autonomous fault response and safe-mode transition under blackout conditions.
Data continuity Buffer, packetize, protect, and return science data without loss when link access is intermittent or delayed. Brain evidence: primary science data return is required within 72 hours via direct-to-Earth or relay.
Environmental tolerance Remain functional through PSR thermal extremes, radiation upsets, dust exposure, and launch or landing shock. Brain evidence: TID risk, thermal cycling, dust contamination, and structural shock are already captured as mission hazards.
Resource & Cost Constraints
Measure Evaluation Rule CDH Trade
Mass and volume Prefer the smallest verified implementation that still supports routing, service access, and required interfaces. A heavier or bulkier option is only justified if it materially improves command resilience or data assurance.
Power and storage Evaluate cruise, descent, checkout, science, downlink, and survival modes as one integrated load profile. Energy per delivered bit and buffer depth both matter under intermittent contact.
Lifecycle cost Include procurement, integration, test, operations, rework, and spares; unresolved values remain `[TBR]`. The lowest-cost option is not acceptable if it weakens data integrity or fault recovery.
Accommodation & Environment
Measure Evaluation Rule CDH Trade
Placement and routing Confirm board placement, connector access, and harness routing do not conflict with lander or rover packaging. Poor placement increases integration risk and can quietly reduce testability.
Communications geometry Verify antenna field-of-regard, terrain masking, and relay or direct-to-Earth access across the nominal attitude profile. The CDH choice must support the planned downlink strategy, not assume ideal geometry.
Thermal, radiation, and dust Assess heat dissipation, shielding, part tolerance, contamination sensitivity, and degradation mechanisms in PSR conditions. Thermal survival and radiation robustness are first-order CDH trades from the Brain notes.
Mission Risk & Maturity
Measure Evaluation Rule CDH Trade
Reliability and fault containment Prefer architectures with watchdogs, safe modes, EDAC, and explicit recovery paths for comms loss or processor upset. Brain evidence: autonomous fault response is required when real-time command is unavailable.
TRL and heritage Favor flight-proven or environment-proven hardware unless a lower-TRL choice closes a documented mission gap. Critical-path CDH functions should not depend on unproven parts without a formal risk case.
Manufacturability Check supplier availability, assembly access, reworkability, and verification flow against the team schedule. If it cannot be built, inspected, and tested in sequence, it should not advance.
Selection rule
When two candidate CDH architectures satisfy the same mission requirements, choose the one with lower verified power per delivered bit, stronger fault containment, simpler verification, and higher environmental maturity. Where the mission baseline has not yet fixed a number, carry it as `[TBR]` rather than inventing a threshold.
